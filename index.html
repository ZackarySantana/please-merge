Glossary

Terms

Merge Queue
A FIFO queue of commits waiting to be validated by CI and merged into the main branch. Commits are processed in order from the HEAD.
Batch / Active Window
The group of commits at the front of the queue that run CI in parallel. Controlled by the "Batch Size" slider.
HEAD
The first (oldest) commit in the queue. Only the HEAD commit can be merged or rejected. All evaluation starts here.
CI (Continuous Integration)
The automated test/build process each commit goes through. Duration and outcome are randomized in the simulator based on your configuration.
Rebase / Restart
When a commit ahead of you fails, your CI results are invalidated and must be re-run from scratch. This is the primary source of wasted CI time.
Speculative Execution
Running CI on multiple commits simultaneously, betting they will all pass. If one fails, the work done on later commits is wasted.
Wall Clock Time
Total simulated time elapsed from start to finish. Batching reduces this compared to running commits one at a time.
Useful CI / Wasted CI
Useful CI is time spent on commits that advanced the queue, including both successful merges and failed commits that were identified and removed. Wasted CI is time thrown away when remaining batch items are restarted after a failure at the head.
Reruns
The number of duplicate CI runs caused by failures forcing the active window to restart. Each rerun adds to wasted CI time.
Waste %
The ratio of wasted CI time to useful CI time. Lower is better. A high waste percentage suggests the batch size may be too large for the success rate.
Optimal Batch Size
Calculated as 1/(1-p) where p is the CI success rate. This is the expected number of commits before a failure, balancing parallelism with waste risk.

Scenarios

HEAD commit succeeds
The commit is merged into main and removed from the queue. The next commit becomes the new HEAD.
Consecutive successes at HEAD
Multiple commits at the front of the queue that all passed CI are merged together in one step. This is the best-case scenario for throughput.
HEAD commit fails
The commit is rejected and moved to the Rejected lane. All remaining commits in the active window have their CI restarted, since their runs were based on the failed commit being present.
Mid-window failure
A commit fails CI but is not yet at HEAD. It waits in the queue showing its red status until all commits before it are processed. When it reaches HEAD, it is rejected.
All commits in batch succeed
The entire active window merges and the next batch of commits from the queue begins CI. This represents maximum efficiency with zero waste.
Queue empty
All commits have been processed (merged or rejected). The simulation ends and the summary is displayed.

Merge Queue Simulator

GitHub's merge queue batches pull requests and tests them together before merging into main. This saves time by running CI in parallel, but when a commit fails, the entire batch restarts.

This interactive simulator lets you experiment with different configurations and see how merge queue behavior changes with varying CI success rates, batch sizes, and durations.

The Three Lanes

Queue

Commits waiting to be tested. The top N form the "active window" and run CI in parallel. The rest wait their turn.

Rejected

Commits whose CI failed. When the head of the queue fails, it's ejected and the remaining active window restarts CI. That's the costly part.

Merged

Commits that passed CI and were merged into main. Consecutive successes at the head are batched together in one merge.

How the Queue Works

1

The first N commits (batch size) run CI simultaneously with random durations.

2

When CI finishes, the queue evaluates from the head (oldest) down.

3

Consecutive successes from the head are merged together.

4

If the head fails, it's rejected and all remaining active commits restart CI, wasting their progress.

This is why a low success rate with a large batch is so expensive. One failure can waste the CI time of many commits.

Batch Size Analysis

The sidebar includes an interactive Batch Size Analysis chart that helps you find the sweet spot for your configuration.

T

Throughput (blue) shows expected merges per cycle before a failure. It peaks at the optimal batch size and drops off as batches get too large.

C

Est. Cost (red) shows CI runs per merged commit. At 100% success rate it's a flat line. As success rate drops, larger batches waste more and more CI.

Click or drag on the chart to set your batch size. The Wall Clock and Est. Cost values below it update in real time based on your settings.

Cost Tracking & Summary

The stats bar at the top tracks useful vs. wasted CI time, wall clock, waste percentage, and time saved compared to running commits sequentially.

$

The Summary page (top bar button) shows full metrics after the simulation ends, including a cost calculator where you set your CI rate and runner count to estimate real-world costs.

↻

After resetting and running again, the summary shows a comparison of your previous run vs. the current one, so you can see the impact of each change.
